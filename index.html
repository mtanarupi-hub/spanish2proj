Las Profesiones

💻 el/la ingeniero/a engineer
«Tanarupi sueña con ser ingeniera de software.»Tanarupi dreams of being a software engineer.

«La periodista escribió un artículo importante.»The journalist wrote an important article.
🔬 el/la científico/a scientist
«Tanarupi es científica; investiga el cambio climático.»Tanarupi is a scientist; she researches climate change.
🍳 el/la cocinero/a cook / chef
«El cocinero prepara tamales para la fiesta.»The cook prepares tamales for the party.
💼 el/la empresario/a entrepreneur
«Malvika fundó su empresa; es una gran empresaria.»Malvika founded her company; she is a great entrepreneur.
🎭 el actor / la actriz actor / actress
«Niral participó en la obra — tiene talento de actor.»Niral participated in the school play — he has actor talent.
🏥 el/la enfermero/a nurse
«El enfermero cuida a los pacientes con dedicación.»The nurse cares for patients with dedication.
💡 Gramática rápida: Say ser + profesión WITHOUT an article when no adjective is present: «Soy médico.» — not «Soy un médico.» (But with adjective: «Soy un médico excelente.» ✓)

El Futuro Simple

What will happen

Formation Formation

Take the infinitive of any verb and add these endings. Irregular verbs use a different stem but the same endings.

In context

📍 Scene: Last day of school — friends imagine their futures
Niral, Tanarupi, and Malvika sit on the steps outside, imagining what comes next.
— Yo seré médico y trabajaré en un hospital de la ciudad.
I will be a doctor and I will work in a city hospital.
— Niral
— Pues yo estudiaré ingeniería. Después, diseñaré puentes increíbles.
Well, I will study engineering. Afterward, I will design incredible bridges.
— Tanarupi
— Y yo abriré mi propio negocio. Tendré empleados y viajaré por el mundo.
And I will open my own business. I will have employees and I will travel the world.
— Malvika
— Los tres tendremos éxito. ¡Celebraremos juntos en diez años!
All three of us will be successful. We will celebrate together in ten years!
— All three
💡 Also used for conjecture: «¿Dónde estará Niral?» = "I wonder where Niral is / Where could Niral be?"

El Futuro Perfecto

What will have happened by a point in time

Formation Formation

Combine the future tense of haber with a past participle. Describes an action completed before a future point in time.

haber (future) + participio pasado
PronounHaber (future)Example

In context

📍 Scene: Graduation night — imagining the 10-year reunion
«Para el año 2035...» — they each make predictions about what they'll have accomplished.
Para el año 2035, Niral ya habrá terminado la residencia médica y habrá atendido a miles de pacientes.
By 2035, Niral will have already finished his medical residency and will have seen thousands of patients.
Para cuando nos reunamos, Tanarupi habrá construido su primer puente y habrá publicado tres artículos científicos.
By the time we meet again, Tanarupi will have built her first bridge and will have published three scientific articles.
Malvika nos dice: «Para entonces, habré abierto cinco tiendas y habré viajado a veinte países.»
Malvika tells us: "By then, I will have opened five stores and will have traveled to twenty countries."
— ¿Habremos cumplido nuestros sueños? — pregunta Tanarupi. — ¡Por supuesto! — responden todos.
"Will we have fulfilled our dreams?" asks Tanarupi. "Of course!" everyone responds.
💡 Key triggers: Look for para + time expression («para el lunes», «para cuando llegues») or ya (already) as signals to use the future perfect.

El Pasado de Subjuntivo

Doubt, desire, and emotion in the past

Formation Formation

Take the ellos/ellas preterite form, drop -ron, and add the past subjunctive endings. Works for all verbs including irregulars!

preterite ellos stem + ra / ras / ra / ramos / rais / ran
PronounPast Subj. FormMeaning
yohablara(that) I spoke
túhablaras(that) you spoke
él/ellahablara(that) he/she spoke
nosotroshabláramos(that) we spoke
vosotroshablarais(that) you all spoke
ellos/Uds.hablaran(that) they spoke

WEIRDO Triggers

The past subjunctive appears after que when the main verb is past (preterite/imperfect/conditional) and expresses:

Wishes
querer, desear, ojalá
Emotion
alegrarse, sentir, temer
Impersonal
era importante que...
Recommend
recomendar, aconsejar
Doubt/Denial
dudar, no creer
Ojalá
I wish / If only

In context

📍 Scene: As adults, looking back on their school years
El profesor quería que Niral estudiara medicina porque era muy inteligente.
The teacher wanted Niral to study medicine because he was very intelligent.
🔑 Wish — quería que + past subjunctive
Era importante que Tanarupi tomara clases de matemáticas avanzadas.
It was important for Tanarupi to take advanced math classes.
🔑 Impersonal expression — era importante que + past subjunctive
Malvika se alegró de que sus padres la apoyaran cuando abrió su negocio.
Malvika was glad that her parents supported her when she opened her business.
🔑 Emotion — se alegró de que + past subjunctive
Ojalá que todos nosotros pudiéramos viajar juntos a España aquel verano.
If only we could all travel together to Spain that summer.
🔑 Ojalá + past subjunctive (past wish/regret)
Los padres de Niral dudaban de que él fuera médico tan joven, pero lo logró.
Niral's parents doubted that he would be a doctor so young, but he made it.
🔑 Doubt — dudaban de que + past subjunctive
💡 Sequence of tenses: Main verb past → subordinate must use past subjunctive. «Quería que estudiara» ✓ — not «Quería que estudie» ✗

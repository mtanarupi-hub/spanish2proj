02

El Futuro Simple

What will happen -- predictions, plans, and conjecture about the present.

How to form it Formation

Take the infinitive of any verb and attach the future endings directly. No stem change is needed for regular verbs. Irregular verbs use a modified stem but the same endings throughout.

infinitivo + e / as / a / emos / eis / an

In context

Scene -- Last day of school
Three friends sit on the front steps and imagine what their lives will look like after graduation.
"Yo sere medico y trabajare en un hospital de la ciudad."
I will be a doctor and I will work in a city hospital.
-- Carlos
"Pues yo estudiare ingenieria. Despues, disenare puentes increibles."
Well, I will study engineering. Afterward, I will design incredible bridges.
-- Ana
"Yo abrire mi propio negocio. Tendre empleados y viajare por el mundo."
I will open my own business. I will have employees and I will travel the world.
-- Sofia
"Los tres tendremos exito. Celebraremos juntos en diez anos."
All three of us will be successful. We will celebrate together in ten years.
-- All three
Also used for conjecture: The future tense can express a guess about something happening right now. "Donde estara Maria?" means "I wonder where Maria is" or "Where could Maria be?"
03

El Futuro Perfecto

What will have already happened by a certain point in the future.

How to form it Formation

Combine the future tense of haber with a past participle. The result describes an action that will be finished before some future moment in time.

haber (future) + participio pasado
PronounHaber (future)Example

In context

Scene -- Graduation night
The same three friends look ahead to their ten-year reunion and predict what each of them will have accomplished by then.
Para el ano 2035, Carlos ya habra terminado la residencia medica y habra atendido a miles de pacientes.
By 2035, Carlos will have already finished his medical residency and will have seen thousands of patients.
Para cuando nos reunamos, Ana habra construido su primer puente y habra publicado tres articulos cientificos.
By the time we meet again, Ana will have built her first bridge and will have published three scientific articles.
Sofia nos dice: "Para entonces, habre abierto cinco tiendas y habre viajado a veinte paises."
Sofia tells us: "By then, I will have opened five stores and will have traveled to twenty countries."
"Habremos cumplido nuestros suenos?" pregunta Ana. "Por supuesto," responden todos.
"Will we have fulfilled our dreams?" asks Ana. "Of course," everyone responds.
Key trigger words: Look for para + a time expression ("para el lunes," "para cuando llegues") or the word ya (already) as signals that the future perfect is the right choice.
04

El Pasado de Subjuntivo

Expressing doubt, desire, emotion, and recommendation about past situations.

How to form it Formation

Start with the ellos / ellas preterite form of any verb. Drop the -ron ending. Then add the past subjunctive endings. This works for regular and irregular verbs alike, because any irregularity from the preterite carries right over.

preterite ellos stem + ra / ras / ra / ramos / rais / ran

ellos / Uds.hablaran(that) they spoke

When to use it -- WEIRDO

The past subjunctive appears in subordinate clauses after "que" when the main verb is in the preterite, imperfect, or conditional, and when the sentence falls into one of these categories:

Wishesquerer, desear, ojala
Emotionalegrarse, sentir, temer
Impersonalera importante que...
Recommendrecomendar, aconsejar
Doubt / Denialdudar, no creer
OjalaI wish / If only

In context

Scene -- Looking back
Now adults, the friends reflect on what teachers and family hoped, expected, and doubted during their school years.
El profesor queria que Carlos estudiara medicina porque era muy inteligente.
The teacher wanted Carlos to study medicine because he was very intelligent.
Wish -- queria que + past subjunctive
Era importante que Ana tomara clases de matematicas avanzadas.
It was important for Ana to take advanced math classes.
Impersonal expression -- era importante que + past subjunctive
Sofia se alegro de que sus padres la apoyaran cuando abrio su negocio.
Sofia was glad that her parents supported her when she opened her business.
Emotion -- se alegro de que + past subjunctive
Ojala que todos nosotros pudieramos viajar juntos a Espana aquel verano.
If only we could all travel together to Spain that summer.
Ojala + past subjunctive (past wish / regret)
Los padres de Carlos dudaban de que el fuera medico tan joven, pero lo logro.
Carlos's parents doubted that he would become a doctor so young, but he made it.
Doubt -- dudaban de que + past subjunctive
Sequence of tenses rule: When the main verb is in the past, the subordinate subjunctive must also be in the past. "Queria que estudiara" is correct. "Queria que estudie" is not.
